=== Question 1 ===
==== Define Encapsulation ====
#Answer below

Encapsulation is the method of binding attributes and methods together to create a class, while hiding the internal state and restricting direct access.

Use cases:
    It is best to use encapsulation when dealing with problems/classes that have sensitive information or internal logic that should not be directly altered by other classes
    This is achieved by using access modifiers such as "private" so that certain attributes can only be accessed or modified through public methods (getters/setters)


=== Question 2 ===
==== Give one advantage of Encapsulation ====
#Answer below

The advantage of Encapsulation is increased security and control, preventing outside classes from accidentally modifying or corrupting an object's internal data.

=== Difference between: "git merge" and "git rebase" ===
#Answer below

"git merge"
    - Combines the history of two branches by creating a brand new "merge commit" that ties them together

"git rebase" 
    - Rewrites the project history by moving a feature branch so it begins at the tip of the main branch so that it creates a linear timeline

=== Question 16 ===
=== For each principle:
    "Encapsulation"
    "Inheritance"
    "Polymorphism"
    "Abstraction"
    Provide:
        - Definition
        - Real-world example ===
#Answer below

Encapsulation - method binding data and functions together to create a single unit called a class and restricting direct outside access to protect it
    - A bank wraps various kinds of a person's information to keep/create their bank account and only allows access via trusted tellers and ATMs

Inheritance - a principle where a subclass (child) automatically gets the attributes and methods of a superclass (parent)
    - A Car and a Motorbike inherit basic traits like "wheels" and "speed" from a parent category called Vehicle  

Polymorphism - ability for different objects to respond to the same action/method in their own unique way 
    - A remote's "On/Off" command sends a signal but the connected device decides how to execute the command
        - TV turns on screen
        - AC blows cold air 

Abstraction - when you use a class/method to show essential features without showing the complex details/inner workings to the user
    - A car is driven by people who have a basic idea of how it works without getting into all the complex details of why it works
