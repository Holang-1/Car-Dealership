=== Question 16 ===
=== For each principle:
    "Encapsulation"
    "Inheritance"
    "Polymorphism"
    "Abstraction"
    Provide:
        - Definition
        - Real-world example ===
#Answer below

Encapsulation - method binding data and functions together to create a single unit called a class 
    - A bank wraps various kinds of a person's information to keep/create their bank account

Inheritance - method of a subclass (child) taking and using attributes and methods of a superclass (parent) by means of overriding
    - A bmw car has similar identifiers with a mercedes car because they are both vehicles sharing commonalities  

Polymorphism - ability for different objects to respond to the same instruction in their own way by means of method overloading 
    - A remote's "On/Off" command sends a signal but the connected device decides how to execute the command
        - TV turns on or Off
        - AC blows cold air 

Abstraction - when you use a class/method to show essential features without showing the inner workings of the class/method
    - A car is driven by people who have a basic idea of how it works without getting into all the complex details of why it works
